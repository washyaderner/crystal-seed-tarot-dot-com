THE FOUR SUITS, AN OVERVIEW

As you learn tarot, what you are really learning is how to break up
and digest everything you experience in life. Without a way to sort
it, your feelings and your experiences pile up and get overwhelming.
The suits are the sorting system. Every experience lands on one of
four planes, and each plane has a suit and an element.

Pentacles are Earth, the physical plane. Work, career, money, home,
health, your body, where you live, the actual making and doing of
things. The people, places, and things of your life.

Cups are Water, the emotional plane. Feelings, love, relationships,
dreams, art, music, the deep parts of yourself, the subconscious.
Anything you hold a special place for in your heart.

Swords are Air, the mental plane. Thoughts, communication of every
kind, decisions, boundaries. Texts, emails, lawsuits, secrets, lies,
the thought loops you run. Swords are the busiest suit because the
mind touches everything.

Wands are Fire, the spirit plane. Passion, motivation, will, energy,
belief, purpose. What fires you up, the fire in your belly, what you
are willing to fight for.

Here is the thing to hold loosely, though: the suits are not black and
white, and they are not supposed to be. One thing can live in more
than one suit at once. A new relationship is Cups and Pentacles both.
That looseness is a feature, not a flaw. It is what keeps a reading
open instead of boxed in.

PENTACLES, EARTH

Pentacles are the Earth suit, and they cover everything in your
physical life. Work, career, hobbies, money, home, health, your body,
your location. Pentacles are the people, places, and things of your
world, and they include movement, travel, making, doing, and turning
an idea into something real you can touch.

When a Pentacles card shows up as a person, upright they tend to be
hardworking, grounded, stable, organized, good with their hands. They
get things done in the real world. Reversed, that same energy can tip
into too materialistic, overworked, stubborn, or rigid, so married to
the physical that they cannot bend.

You will start to recognize Pentacles people in your own life. The
interior decorator. The makeup artist. The CEO. The builder. The Queen
of Pentacles is my favorite of the bunch, the "boss bitch" who has her
physical world completely handled and does not need a thing from
anyone.

CUPS, WATER

Cups are the Water suit, and they are your emotional life. Your
feelings, your loves, your desires, your dreams, your relationships,
and the things you hold a special place for in your heart. Cups reach
into familial bonds, hidden talents, the deepest parts of you, and the
subconscious. Because Cups are about what you love, they also carry
beauty, art, music, creativity, and imagination.

As people, the upright Cups are warm, loving, compassionate,
nurturing, the empath who feels everything. The healer, the artist,
the musician, the friend who holds space for everyone. Reversed, those
same cups get poured out. The person neglects their own self-care,
runs on empty, gets depressed or emotionally stunted, and at the very
worst can turn that emotional power into something that hurts.

Cups are deep water. Beautiful when they flow, heavy when they
stagnate. Learn to feel the difference.

SWORDS, AIR

Swords are the Air suit, and they are one of the most diverse elements
in the deck because they cover the whole life of the mind. Your
thoughts, your intellect, your communication, your boundaries, your
decisions. Being like-minded with someone. The thought loops and
patterns you run. And communication of every single kind, from a
tender conversation with yourself all the way out to emails, texts,
job offers, lawsuits, secrets, and lies.

The fastest way to understand Swords is to picture the sword itself,
because a sword is a tool and a weapon at the same time. You can raise
it in the air, point it where you want to go, and yell "CHARGE." You
can plant it in the ground and draw a line in the sand to say this is
my boundary and it does not get crossed. You can use it to cut a toxic
person or situation out of your life for your own good. So think of
the sword as a decision-maker. It cuts ties with what no longer serves
you, it points where you aim your energy, and it draws the boundaries
that keep you from bleeding your energy dry.

As people, the upright Swords are smart, capable, witty, good
communicators, clear decision-makers. The lawyer, the judge, the
teacher, the strategist. Reversed, that sharp mind turns on itself or
on others: the person who cannot speak their truth, the overthinker,
the anxious one, the sharp tongue that cuts when it does not mean to.

WANDS, FIRE

Wands are the Fire suit, and they are pure energy. Motivation, spirit,
passion, will, inspiration. The way you direct your energy, and that
energy itself.

Here is the distinction that keeps Wands and Swords straight: think of
Wands as your personality and Swords as your mind. Wands are fire, so
think of them as your own fire-brand. When you want to find the Wands
energy in a situation, ask "what fires you up?" or "where is the fire
in your belly?" This is your sheer will, your life-force, what you
believe in, what gives you purpose, what you want to pour your energy
into next.

And because Wands are fire, remember the flames of passion can be love
or fighting. We can be passionate about something we adore and
passionate about something we will go to war over. So Wands also show
what you believe and what you are willing to fight for.

As people, upright Wands have big energy. Inspiring, magnetic, the
leader who makes something out of nothing. Reversed, the fire goes one
of two ways: too much (bossy, quick-tempered, burning everyone around
them) or burned out (the candle lit at both ends, nothing left). My
favorite card in the whole deck is the 8 of Wands, eight wands flying
freely across the sky with no human element in the card at all, not
even a cloud hand. Pure magic, moving on its own.

THE SUITS SORT (THE WORKSHEET AND QUIZ)

This is where the suits stop being four definitions and start being a
tool you can actually use. The exercise looks like a sorting quiz, but
the catch is the whole lesson: most of these words live in more than
one suit, and your job is not to be "right." Your job is to defend
where you put it.

Take creativity. Is it Cups, drawing on your deepest waters and your
feeling? Is it Wands, the spark and the drive to carry an idea out? Is
it Swords, because an idea itself is a thought, and the written word
is communication? Or is it Pentacles, the actual making and doing that
gets a creative thing off the ground? The honest answer is that it can
be any of them, and a good reader can say why for whichever one they
pick.

Same with the law. Swords, because it is the mind, the argument, the
contract, the boundary? Or Pentacles, because it is power over your
physical reality, the judge who can change your real life? Defend it.

A reader who can explain why a card lives in two places at once is
reading. A reader who only knows one box per word is reciting. We are
building readers here. After the sort, give yourself a one-card
reading: pull a single card, name its suit, and read what plane of
life it is pointing at, before you look anything up.
