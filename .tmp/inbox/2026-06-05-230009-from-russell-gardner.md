TAROTDOXA
MINI CROSS

present: Queen of Wands
crossing: Seven of Wands
root: Knight of Pentacles
past: Three of Swords
on_your_mind: The Sun (Reversed)
near_future: King of Wands



Overview

Five out of six cards upright with only one reversal. That's a strong hand,
and the one reversal is doing almost all the heavy lifting in this reading,
so we're going to give it the attention it deserves.

Now here's what jumped out at me immediately: you are on fire. Three wands
out of six cards, and all three are upright. Wands are the fire suit. What
fires you up, what's the fire in your belly, where your energy and passion
live. And you didn't just pull a couple of wands, you pulled the Queen, the
Seven, and the King. That's the Queen and King of the suit plus a challenge
card sandwiched between them. You have mastered fire energy. You know who
you are, you know what drives you, and you are fighting like hell to
protect it. This is not someone who lacks motivation or direction. This is
someone burning bright.

But here's what's missing, and it's loud: not a single cup in this entire
spread. Zero. Cups are the water suit, our ability to give and receive
love, emotional fulfillment, all the feelings stuff. You've got fire for
days and absolutely no water to balance it. You're burning without feeling.
Or more accurately, you're burning instead of feeling. All that fire energy
is real and powerful, but fire without water eventually just scorches
everything, including you.

The one reversed card? The Sun, sitting in your "on your mind" position.
Happiness after a long period of darkness, but it's upside down. The thing
you're thinking about most is the joy you can't quite access yet. That
reversal is the hinge of this whole reading.

Present - Queen of Wands

Here you are. The fun queen, the witchy queen, the unapologetic queen.
Black cat at her feet, sunflowers everywhere, "this is me and I don't
apologize for it" energy. She sits on her throne with total confidence in
who she is. You know your worth, you know your power, and you're not
dimming yourself for anyone.

This is a beautiful card to have in your present position. It tells me that
wherever you're standing right now, you're standing there fully as
yourself. No mask, no performance, no shrinking. That's not nothing. A lot
of people never get to this seat.

Crossing - Seven of Wands

So here's what's crossing you, the energy being created in relation to that
Queen energy. The Seven of Wands is the whack-a-mole card. Look at this guy
standing on his craggy little hill, one against six, and he didn't even put
on matching shoes this morning because he was too busy fighting before
breakfast. Seven is a challenge number, and when you have an upright seven,
you rise to the occasion. You're winning this fight. You have the high
ground.

But the crossing position tells me this is what's complicating your Queen
of Wands confidence right now. You're having to defend who you are, defend
your position, defend your choices. Constantly. One thing after another
after another. You're more than capable of handling it, clearly, but notice
that this is fire crossing fire. You're fighting with the same energy
you're living with. There's no cool-down period. It's passion defending
passion, and that's exhausting even when you're winning.

Root - Knight of Pentacles

Down at the foundation, the root under everything, we've got the Knight of
Pentacles. He rides the slowest horse in the deck. This guy is not flashy.
He's not galloping anywhere. He's methodical, reliable, patient. He holds
up his pentacle but doesn't even look at it because the work is just what
he does. Pentacles are the earth suit, the physical world. Job, money,
home, health, adulting.

This is the bedrock you built all that fire on top of. At some point you
learned to just put your head down and do the work. Show up, be
responsible, be useful, be steady. And that patience, that reliability,
became the foundation for everything above it. But here's the thing I want
to ask you honestly. Your patience is legendary. Is it a tool you're using,
or has it become a weapon other people use against you? Because there's a
version of this Knight where patience stops being a virtue and starts being
the reason you tolerate things way longer than you should.

This Knight sits right next to your Three of Swords in the past, and those
two elements, earth and air, grind against each other. The slow steady
patience of this foundation fed directly into a painful moment.

Past - Three of Swords

Three swords through a heart with rain pouring down behind it. This is the
painful creation card. Three is the number of creation and alchemy, and
swords are how we carve our way in life, our decisions and communication.
So this is creation through pain. The breakup, the friendship that ended,
the conversation that cut deep, the truth that had to be spoken even though
it wrecked something.

This happened. Upright, it means you did the painful thing. You didn't
avoid it, you didn't stuff it down. You went through it with your eyes
open, and it hurt exactly as much as it looks like it hurt. Rain and clouds
and a pierced heart. No sugarcoating that.

And then look at what you did. You took that pain and you climbed right
back onto that Queen of Wands throne. You went from heartbreak straight
into "this is who I am and I don't apologize." That takes something. But
I'll tell you what I notice you skipped: the grieving part. The water part.
The sitting-with-the-feelings part. You went from swords to wands without
passing through cups, and the total absence of cups in this spread is
confirming that pattern. You processed the pain by fighting through it, not
by feeling through it.

On Your Mind - The Sun Reversed

Here's your one reversal, and it's sitting right on top of your head in the
"what you've been thinking about" position. The Sun is happiness after a
long period of darkness. Upright, it's the card that says the storm is over
and the light came back. Reversed, that happiness is dimmed. It's not gone.
It's just harder to access, like the sun is behind clouds and you know it's
there but you can't feel the warmth on your skin yet.

This is your homework card. Even though it's not in the future position,
it's reversed in the place that represents your mental space, which means
this is where the work lives. You're thinking about happiness. You're
thinking about joy. But something is blocking you from actually letting it
in, and if I look at this whole spread, I think I know what it is. You have
all this fire, all this fight, all this capability, and zero emotional
reception happening. The Sun reversed here isn't telling you that happiness
isn't coming. It's telling you that you can't receive it while your hands
are full of wands. You're going to have to put one down.

This is that male energy versus female energy thing. Male energy is go, do,
fix, fight, protect, hustle. Female energy is sit, receive, reflect, let it
land. You are running at about 95/5 right now, almost entirely in the
doing-and-fighting mode. The Sun can't come back to full brightness until
you make room to actually feel the warmth when it shows up.

Near Future - King of Wands

The direction already in motion. The King of Wands. He sits on his throne
with a flowering wand, lion on the back of his seat, cap of maintenance
under his crown. This is mastered fire energy, fully manifested. If the
Queen is "I know who I am," the King is "and now everyone else knows too."

Interesting that you've got the Queen in your present and the King in your
near future. That's a progression. Court cards often represent real people,
and the King of Wands could absolutely be someone showing up in your life.
A fiery, passionate, honest person. Someone who matches your energy. Or
this could be you stepping into an even bigger version of yourself. The
Queen and King of Wands together are the artsy couple energy, big
mastered-fire, unapologetic, possibly-dye-their-hair-purple energy.

But here's what I keep coming back to. Your near future is more fire. Your
present is fire. Your crossing is fire. Fire on fire on fire. This King
arriving is a good thing, truly. But if you meet this energy, whether it's
a person or a new chapter of your own power, without having done the water
work first? You're going to burn together instead of warming each other.
The best version of this King showing up is you greeting him from a place
where your cup is full, not where your cup doesn't even exist in the
reading.

---

What This Reading Is Saying

You are powerful, capable, and unapologetically yourself. That is not in
question. The cards see it, I see it, and honestly you probably don't need
anyone to tell you that. What you need someone to tell you is this: you are
allowed to stop fighting long enough to feel something.

That Three of Swords in your past was real pain. And you handled it the way
a fire person handles things: you stood up, you climbed your hill, you
grabbed your wand, and you kept going. That survival instinct is the reason
you're still standing. But surviving and thriving are different things, and
right now that reversed Sun is telling you the joy is right there waiting,
you're just too busy swinging at things to notice it landing.

Your homework is water. I know that sounds vague so let me be specific. I
want you to journal about that Three of Swords. What happened, how it
actually felt, not how you managed it or what you did next, but how it felt
in your body. There is something magical between your hand and your brain,
and when the feelings get out on paper, they stop circulating in your
chest. Second, I want you to ask yourself what your version of receiving
looks like. A bath, a long walk with no podcast, sitting somewhere quiet
and letting yourself just exist without producing or protecting anything.
Whatever it is, do it, and do it without calling it lazy.

You have a King of Wands heading your way, and whether that's a person or a
new phase of your own power, you want to meet it with a full cup, not just
a blazing torch. The fire is handled. The fire has been handled. Now fill
the cup, and let that Sun turn right side up.
