THE INTRO PEOPLE READ WHEN MODULE 1 OPENS

Before you memorize a single card meaning, I need you to know something. You've been doing this your whole life. Every time you walked into a room and felt the vibe shift, every time you looked at someone and knew they weren't telling the truth, every time your gut said something and you didn't listen and then wished you had? That's the same skill. This module is about proving to yourself that you already have it.

LESSON 1: YOU ALREADY KNOW MORE THAN YOU THINK
(Video: you, warm, the whole philosophy in one sitting.)

Here is the thing nobody tells you when you buy your first deck. You do not have to memorize seventy-eight cards to start reading. You already read all day long. You read the room when you walk into work and something is off. You read a friend's face and know the thing they are saying is not the thing they mean. You read a street and know it is not one to walk down at night. That is intuition, and you have been running it your whole life.

Tarot is not a test you pass by memorizing. It is a practice of learning to trust what you already sense, and then giving it language. There is no single right answer in a reading. There are many right answers, because your subconscious knows things, and the collective consciousness we all draw from knows things, and the cards are full of images and symbols that are already sitting in your awareness. Two good readers can read the same spread differently and both be right.

So for this whole first stretch, I am going to ask you to do something that feels backward: read the card before you look up what it means. Trust the first thing you see. We will add the book knowledge later, and it will make you sharper. But if you start with the book, you will talk yourself out of the truest thing you noticed. We lead with the knowing. The studying comes second.

LESSON 2: THE THREE CLAIRS
(Video: the three ways a message reaches you, kept concrete.)

When a card speaks to you, it comes in through one of three doors. Most people have a favorite door, the one that opens first, and part of learning to read is finding yours and then learning to use the other two.

The first is clairvoyance, which just means clear seeing. You look at a card and get a picture in your head, or two cards make a bigger image together, or something in the art grabs your eye and will not let go. If you are a visual person, this is probably your door.

The second is clairaudience, clear hearing. You look at a card and a word, a phrase, or an old saying pops up. A silver lining. No good deed goes unpunished. Burn it down and start over. I have had a single phrase carry an entire reading. If words are your thing, this is your door.

The third is clairsentience, clear feeling, and it is the one people skip and it might be the most important. You look at a card and you just FEEL something. Heavy. Light. Uneasy. Hopeful. You do not have a picture or a phrase, you have a body response. Sometimes simply describing the feeling you are getting is enough to make the whole reading land for the person across from you.

You do not need all three firing at once. You need to know which one is yours, trust it, and slowly invite the other two in.

(Then they pull a card and note which clair fired first.)

LESSON 3: WORD ASSOCIATION WARM-UP
(Video: walk through the word-association idea live, holding up colors and numbers.)

This is the lesson that proves the whole point of Level 1. You are going to look at colors, numbers, and symbols, the raw ingredients of every card, and write down what they bring up for you. No looking anything up. No right answers. Just your gut.

Here is the move I want you to learn, because it is the move I make in every single reading. I never cross out an answer. I add to it. You say red means romance? Good. It does. And it also means the root chakra, your base and your survival. And it also means blood, and life force. And it also means anger, or a warning. All of those are true at the same time, and the cards sitting around the red card tell you which one is loudest today.

Same with a number. You say two means a couple? Good. And also opposites. And also a choice, a fork in the road. And also two things that need to be balanced. You are not memorizing one meaning per symbol. You are building a little web of meaning around each one, so that when you see it in a card, you have options to choose from instead of one rigid definition to cling to.

That is what makes a reading feel alive instead of stiff. We keep it, in my words, loosey-goosey.

(Then they fill out the Word Associations worksheet and post their favorites.)

LESSON 4: FIRST IMPRESSIONS, READING ONE CARD BY FEEL
(Video: you pull a card and read it with the psychic side only, out loud.)

Now we put the last three lessons together into your first real reading move: the first-impression read. This is reading a single card with the psychic side only, before any studying.

When the card turns over, run these in order, fast, without overthinking. What strikes you first? The very first thing your eye or your gut grabs. What are you drawn to in the picture? A figure, a color, an object, a direction. Does it remind you of anything, or make a phrase pop up? What do you FEEL?

Then say it out loud, or write it down, in one go. A shoot-from-the-hip read. Do not edit it. Do not check it. The discipline here is trusting the first hit and not talking yourself out of it, because the moment you start second-guessing is the moment you reach for the book and lose the thread.

You will be wrong sometimes. That is fine. Being wrong out loud and surviving it is how you build the nerve to read for other people later. For now, you are only reading for yourself, so there is no one to impress and nothing to lose. Just the card, and your first true response to it.

(Then they do a shoot-from-the-hip read and post it.)

LESSON 5: THE TWO SIDES, PSYCHIC PLUS BOOK LEARNING
(Video: the master cheat sheet, psychic impressions on one side, book learning on the other.)

Everything in this course lives on this one page, so keep it close. There are two sides to reading tarot, and you use both hands every time.

The psychic side is what we have been training all level. First impressions. What you are drawn to. The phrases that pop up. The feelings. How the cards relate to each other. The overall picture the whole spread is painting. You lead with this hand.

The book side is what we learn next. Colors. Numbers. Suits and elements. Reversals. Astrology. Symbols. And the repeats, more than one throne, more than one horse, more than one set of pillars, which always mean something. You layer this hand in second.

Here is the order, and it matters: psychic first, book second. Always. If you start with the book, you walk in with a definition and force the card to fit it. If you start with the psychic side, you notice the true thing first, and then the book knowledge sharpens and confirms it. The book never overrides your gut. It serves it.

From here on, every lesson is going to hand you more of the book side, the suits, the numbers, the court, the Majors, the reversals, the spreads. As we go, keep this sheet next to you and remember it is the second hand, not the first. You already have the first one.
