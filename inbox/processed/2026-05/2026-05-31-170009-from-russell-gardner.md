Overview

Alright, let me take a look at what we've got here.

Seven out of ten cards are upright, which is honestly a solid ratio. You're

about them.

Here's what jumps out at me first: this spread is dominated by wands. We've
got the Ten of Wands in your present, the King of Wands reversed in your
near future, the Four of Wands in your hopes and fears, the Four of Cups as
future energy (which carries its own weight), and the Five of Wands
reversed as your outcome. Wands are the fire suit. What fires you up,
what's the fire in your belly, where your energy and motivation go. When I
see this many wands, the reading is fundamentally about your energy and
what you've been spending it on. And that Ten of Wands sitting right in
your present position tells me the answer is: everything. You've been
spending it on everything.

We've also got three major arcana cards. The Devil, Justice, and The Sun.
That's the universe side of the deck showing up alongside all this earthly
fire energy, which tells me the universe is involved here, co-creating with
you, and paying attention. You're working with the universe the right way.
This isn't crisis energy from the majors. This is the universe saying "I
see what you're carrying, and I have something to say about it."

One more thing before we dig in. There's exactly one cup in this whole
spread, and it's reversed. One sword, upright. No pentacles at all. The
emotional layer of your life is sitting at the bottom of the pile right
now, reversed and struggling, while all of your fire is burning hot. That
imbalance is going to be a big part of what we talk about.

Present - Ten of Wands

So here you are. The harvest card. This is the guy carrying ten massive
wands stacked up in his arms, staggering toward a town in the distance, and
he can barely see over the top of them. Ten is the end of a chapter. You've
been working your ass off, and you have something to show for it. That part
is real and worth acknowledging. Whatever you've been building, grinding
on, pouring yourself into, it produced results.

But look at this guy. He's not celebrating. He's not high-fiving anybody.
He's hunched over under the weight of his own success, and he can't even
see where he's going anymore. The harvest came in, and instead of putting
some of it down, you picked up ALL of it. Every single wand. Every
responsibility, every project, every thing that needed doing. You said
"I'll carry it" so many times that now you can barely walk.

This is your present moment. You are at the end of a fire chapter, loaded
down with everything you've built, and the weight has become the problem.

Crossing - The Devil

Now here's what's crossing you, the energy being generated in relation to
that overloaded present. I don't do scary tarot. If something looks scary,
I'll tell you what it actually means.

The Devil is about patterns you know aren't serving you but haven't broken
yet. Could be a habit, a way of thinking, a dynamic with someone, a
relationship with work itself. Look at the card. Those two figures chained
to the Devil's altar? The chains are loose around their necks. They could
take them off any time. That's the whole point of this card. It's not that
you're trapped. It's that you've convinced yourself you are.

Crossed with the Ten of Wands, this is telling me you are chained to the
carrying. You have built an identity around being the one who does it all,
handles it all, shows up for everything. And that pattern has become its
own kind of bondage. You know you're burning out. You know this isn't
sustainable. But some part of you believes that if you put the wands down,
everything falls apart. That's the Devil's lie. The chains are loose. You
could set some of this down, but you haven't yet because the pattern feels
safer than the unknown.

Root - Ace of Swords

Underneath all of this, at the very foundation, is the Ace of Swords.
Swords are how we carve our way in life. Mentality, communication,
decisions, boundaries. An ace is a one. Brand new beginning, the seed of
something. And this is a hand bursting out of a cloud holding a crowned
sword.

At your root, this tells me there was a moment, maybe a long time ago,
where you made a sharp, clear decision. You carved a path for yourself. You
decided what kind of person you were going to be, what you were going to
build, how you were going to show up. That decision was powerful and
triumphant. The crown on the sword says so. But that single foundational
decision, that clarity about who you are and what you do, is also what's
underneath the Ten of Wands and the Devil. The roots of "I carry
everything" started with a real, legitimate moment of strength and clarity.
You decided you were capable, and you were right. The problem is that
decision never got updated.

Past - Ace of Cups Reversed

Here's your one and only cup, and it's reversed. Cups are your ability to
give and receive love, emotions, the whole feeling layer of being alive. An
ace is a new beginning, the purest form of that suit's energy. The Ace of
Cups upright is the hand of the universe offering you a cup overflowing
with emotional abundance, a dove descending, water pouring in every
direction.

Reversed? That cup flips. The water pours out. The emotional new beginning
that was offered couldn't land, or got blocked, or you couldn't receive it.

In your recent past, something happened emotionally that either drained you
completely or was a new emotional chapter that you couldn't let yourself
fully step into. With all this fire energy burning everywhere else in the
spread, I think you've been so busy carrying those wands that when love,
connection, or emotional nourishment showed up, you literally didn't have a
free hand to hold the cup. It poured right out. This is the only water in
the entire reading, and it's upside down. Your emotional life has been the
casualty of all that carrying.

On Your Mind - Justice

Justice is number eleven in the major arcana. Eleven is the start of a new
chapter, one step past the completion of ten. She sits between two pillars,
which means when you cross that threshold, you're going to be in a new
version of yourself. She holds a sword in one hand for boundaries and
truth-telling, and scales in the other for fairness.

This is what's been on your mind. You've been thinking about what's fair.
Not in a petty way, but in a deep, structural way. After carrying all those
wands, after being chained to the pattern of doing everything, some part of
you has been doing the math and going "wait. This isn't balanced. I'm doing
all the doing and getting none of the receiving." Justice says you're
right. The scales are off. And the fact that she's a major arcana card
sitting in your "on your mind" position means this isn't a passing thought.
The universe put this realization in your head for a reason. You're
starting to see that the current arrangement isn't equitable, and you're
thinking about what it would look like to demand better.

Near Future - King of Wands Reversed

This is where we need to pay attention. The King of Wands is the fully
mastered fire energy. He's the person who has conquered their drive, their
passion, their creative force and wears it like a crown. He's got a lion on
his throne. He's confident, decisive, and knows exactly how to wield his
fire.

Reversed, that throne starts to crush him. The King of Wands reversed can
be someone whose fire has turned on them. Severe, austere, burned out from
their own intensity. He's still sitting on the throne, but the throne is
winning. The fire that used to fuel him is now consuming him.

In your near future position, this is the direction things are already
heading if nothing changes. All that carrying from the Ten of Wands,
chained to the pattern by the Devil, with a poured-out cup behind you? It
leads to the king of fire getting crushed by his own fire. This could also
represent a person in your life, someone with big fire energy who's become
overbearing or whose intensity is part of what's weighing you down. Either
way, this reversed king is showing you what the current trajectory
produces: more fire, less control, the throne winning.

But here's the thing. This is the near future, not a locked-in destination.
It's the road you're on, and roads have exits.

You Deep Down - The Sun

Oh, I love this card here. The Sun. Happiness after a long period of
darkness. This is a major arcana card sitting in the position of who you
are at your core, underneath everything, and it's upright.

Look at this card. A kid on a white horse, banner flying, sunflowers
blooming, a massive sun shining down. Pure, unfiltered joy. Innocence in
the best sense, not naivety but the kind of wisdom that comes out the other
side of hard things and still knows how to feel happiness.

Deep down, under all those wands you're carrying, under the chains of the
Devil, under the reversed cup and the approaching burnout, you are
fundamentally a person made of light. You know what happiness feels like.
You know what freedom feels like. The Sun in this position is the
universe's way of saying "this person hasn't lost themselves, even if it
feels like it." The kid is still there. The warmth is still there. It's
just buried under ten wands right now.

This card also tells me you've been through darkness before and came out
the other side. For you to have that Sun at your core after everything the
rest of this spread is showing, that's something. That's survival and it's
also proof that you know how to get back to the light.

The marriage card. The celebration card. Four sturdy wands with garlands

actually pays off and you get to enjoy it with people you love.

This is what you're hoping for. You want the harvest to turn into
celebration. You want to arrive at the place where the work becomes a life
you actually enjoy living. And a four is a stable number, the four legs of

But sitting in the hopes and fears position, there might also be a little

worth noticing, because it's connected to everything else we've been

stable in the wrong direction. It's stable dissatisfaction. Comfortable
numbness. You've gotten so used to the grind that even when something new

you've shut that channel down.

Here's where The Sun in your deep-down position matters. The Sun says you
KNOW what joy feels like. The Four of Cups says you've temporarily lost the
ability to recognize it when it's being handed to you. That reversed Ace of
Cups in your past spilled the water, and now you're sitting in front of
cups going "what's even the point." The universe is literally reaching a
hand out of the clouds with something for you, and you're too tired and too
numb to notice.

This is not a permanent state. This is what happens when someone with a Sun
at their core gets buried under ten wands and chained to a pattern. The
boredom isn't who you are. It's what burnout looks like from the inside.

Outcome - Five of Wands Reversed

Five of Wands upright is chaos. A bunch of people swinging sticks around,

The homework here is clear: you need to stop the internal war before it

patterns, all that refusing to receive, it's creating friction that will
eventually blow up sideways if you don't address it directly. The Five of
Wands reversed is the universe saying "send it back. Make my order again. I
ordered this with cheese." You don't have to accept this outcome. But
flipping it upright means actually engaging with the conflict honestly
instead of letting it fester.

Five is the change number. Wands are fire, your energy, what fires you up.
Reversed Five of Wands says your energy has been in a state of confused
conflict. Not the productive "okay let's figure this out" kind, but the
kind where you're spinning and nothing is landing clearly. The fight isn't
visible anymore. It's internal. You're exhausted from a battle nobody else
can see.

Known - Queen of Pentacles Reversed

What you already know about your health situation: Queen of Pentacles
reversed. This is a big one. Upright, she's the queen who has it all
handled. She's nurturing, grounded, abundant, the one who takes care of

Queens are inward power. Pentacles are your physical life, your body. A

normally manages everything on the physical plane is feeling undermined.
You know how to take care of yourself. That's not the problem. The problem

scary. You're used to being able to handle things, and this one isn't
cooperating.

Unknown - Four of Cups

Now here's the card doing the most work in this spread. What you haven't

Advice - The Lovers

Your advice card is The Lovers, and this is the one major arcana in the

In a health reading, The Lovers as advice says: you need to choose your
alignment. This might mean choosing a provider you actually trust and
committing to that relationship fully rather than bouncing between
conflicting opinions. It might mean choosing to be fully honest with
someone about what's going on, whether that's a doctor, a partner, or
yourself. The angel pouring influence down means there is spiritual support

The Lovers is also about the relationship between your body and your mind.
Look at the card. The Tree of Life is behind one figure and the Tree of
Knowledge behind the other. Your body knows things your analytical brain
keeps overriding, and your brain knows things your body is too tired to act
on. The advice is to get those two back in conversation with each other.
Stop letting them operate as separate entities fighting for control. That's
the real alignment this card is asking for.

Nine is the number of inward completion. Integrity, independence, all on
